## Case 1 — normal premature marketplace plan

Case ID: `human-normal-product-critique-001`

### Public-safe input

> Review this plan: launch a paid marketplace in six weeks for small AI teams. Build
> seller onboarding, checkout, ratings and discovery in parallel. Evidence so far is 40
> waitlist signups and five interviews; no team has installed a listed resource twice.
> Success is defined as 100 published resources at launch.

### AI-generated candidate output

#### Strongest next move

Delay the paid marketplace. Run a two-week install-and-reuse MVP with three design teams,
one narrow resource category and manual seller onboarding.

#### What the plan is deciding

- Target user: small AI teams.
- Claimed outcome: a useful paid marketplace.
- Proposed proxy: 100 published resources.
- Available evidence: 40 signups, five interviews and no observed repeat install.

#### Critical weaknesses

1. **The success criterion measures supply, not user value.** Publishing 100 resources
   does not show that teams can find, trust, install or reuse one.
2. **Demand evidence is weak.** A waitlist and interviews establish interest, not paid or
   repeat behavior.
3. **Four systems are coupled before the core loop is proven.** Seller onboarding,
   checkout, ratings and discovery create operational and trust complexity without
   evidence that the install loop works.
4. **Safety and quality gates are absent.** The plan does not say which resources are
   eligible, what a rating means or how unsafe listings are blocked.

#### MVP boundary and success criteria

- One category and at most ten candidate resources.
- Manual onboarding; no money collection or public ratings.
- Measure: each design team completes one intended task and voluntarily reuses the same
  exact release within 14 days.
- Record failed discovery, install and task outcomes separately.
- Proceed to paid-marketplace design only if at least two teams repeat use and no open
  safety blocker remains.

The numerical threshold is a proposed decision rule, not a forecast. The input contains
no retention benchmark or willingness-to-pay evidence.

## Case 2 — ambiguous feature request

Case ID: `human-ambiguous-product-plan-001`

### Public-safe input

> Critique our plan to add AI and tell us the best MVP.

### AI-generated candidate output

There is not enough plan to critique or recommend an MVP. “Add AI” does not identify a
user, behavior, problem, decision or requested outcome.

Please provide:

1. the target user and the job they cannot complete today;
2. the current workflow and observable failure;
3. the AI-assisted behavior being proposed;
4. evidence that the problem occurs often enough to matter;
5. safety, data and latency constraints;
6. one measurable outcome and the smallest reversible test.

Until those exist, choosing a model or building a general assistant would be premature.
The next product move is a problem interview or workflow observation focused on one
repeated task, not feature implementation.
