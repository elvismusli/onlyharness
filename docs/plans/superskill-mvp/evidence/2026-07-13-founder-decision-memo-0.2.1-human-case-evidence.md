### Public-safe input

> Decide whether a two-person product team should build a public marketplace now or
> first ship a four-week private pilot. Known facts: there are 12 selected but unreviewed
> candidates, no approved releases yet, three design partners willing to test, and one engineer can
> support onboarding. The goal is evidence of repeat weekly use before expanding supply.

### AI-generated candidate output

#### Recommendation

Run the four-week private pilot first. Do not build the public marketplace yet.

#### Decision and constraints

- The decision is sequencing: private pilot now versus public marketplace now.
- The stated success target is repeat weekly use, not listing count or public traffic.
- Current supply is not approval-ready, and support capacity is one engineer.
- Three willing design partners provide a bounded way to test the target behavior.

#### Options and tradeoffs

| Option | Speed to relevant evidence | Main risk | Reversibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| Four-week private pilot | High: the three partners can test weekly use directly | Small sample may not generalize | High; pilot workflow and findings can feed a later marketplace |
| Public marketplace now | Low: launch activity would not by itself prove repeat use | Premature trust claims, support load and marketplace work before an approved release | Lower; public expectations and operational surface are harder to unwind |

#### Caveats

The input does not include engineering estimates, partner task mix or a definition of
“repeat weekly use.” Those unknowns prevent a reliable schedule or adoption forecast,
but they do not change which option best matches the stated goal.

#### Next action

Within two working days, define one representative task and one weekly-use criterion for
each design partner. Run the pilot with candidate-only language, record successful and
failed uses, and make the marketplace decision after week four. Escalate earlier only if
no candidate can safely serve any representative task.

### AI-generated candidate output

I cannot make a defensible recommendation from this input. “Growth” and
“profitability” are not defined, and no constraints or real options are supplied.

Please provide:

1. current runway, fixed commitments and the minimum cash buffer;
2. which growth action is actually available and its estimated cost;
3. the profitability lever under consideration and its customer impact;
4. the quarter’s non-negotiable objective and decision deadline;
5. evidence ranges for conversion, retention and payback rather than a single optimistic
   forecast.

write two bounded scenarios with explicit stop conditions. That is a process safeguard,
